About Us

Our Mission

Partners in hope is a local nongovernmental non-profit organization whose mission is to continue the worldwide fight against violence of women and youth who are subsequently victims of impoverished living standards because of the lack of the necessities of food, shelter, clothing, and good health. Partners in hope also recognizes that the opportunities for adequate education are not offered to women and youth. Therefore, through means of educational activities and curriculum, partners in hope dedicated to providing women and youth tools that aim to empower themselves to combat violence and inequality in their daily lives.

Our Project

Partners in hope will address the inequality women and youth face in obtaining basic needs: shelter, food, clothing, and health. Secondly, partners in hope will advocate for females to be empowered to attend and stay in school and desire graduation.

Problem Statement

Unequal power and gender inequality are significant drivers of violence and poverty primarily against women and youth. The consequences of violence against women and youth Childhood Pregnancy, Early Marriage, Physical Injuries, Mental Distress from Trauma and Poverty, School Dropouts, Social Stigmatization, Poor Self-Esteem Target Areas The project will be implemented in that are most subject to poor living and educational conditions. These districts have some of the highest levels of vulnerability as victims of inhumane violence against women and youth.

Our Goal

Partners in hope, through interventions seeks positive behavioural changes in targeted groups. Our aim is to reduce the poverty level and fight violence against women and youth, and to improve shelter, food, clothing, health, and education.

Outcomes

  • Improved educational conditions and curriculum.
  • A voice for women and youth against violence.
  • Storehouses for easy access to food and clothing.
  • A pride in personal well-being.

    • Monitoring and Evaluation

  • To create a Planning System and Monitoring Framework to determine the trends of downward and upward outcomes.
  • To be accountable to communities, partners and donors with quarterly reports regarding the effective use of resources including financial, curriculum and volunteer activities.
  • To report the achievement of expected results.
  • To provide guiding principles that maximise the impact and quality of information.
  • To inform communities of the decision-making process.
  • To regularly report narrative/financial semi-annual reports and annual financial audits.
